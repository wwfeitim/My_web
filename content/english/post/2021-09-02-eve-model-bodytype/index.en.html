Overview

This post is the second part following by my last post. In this post, I include the models and body types and analyse the trends and patterns among the 13 brands.

Due to the matter of time, I only focus on the five brands: BMW, Holden, Mazda, Volkswagen and Toyota. If you interest in other brands, you are welcome to visit my Github. The data I collected is for study only, and no one should use it for commercial purposes. I hope this post can help you to match your budgets and cars, and find your ideal cars for family or commercial use.

At the end of this post, I will also answer the question: Does the dealer price the cars higher?


Summary of brands

Figure 1: Median price of all brands and body types

This figure 1 includes the model years from 2011 to 2021. The median price of body types is lower than 50,000 AUD. There is more than 50% number of cars in the market are less than 50,000 AUD. Hatch, Sedan, and Wagon have the lowest median price among all body types approx. 25,000 AUD. However, there are vital changes if we only focus on the MY20 and MY21.


Figure 2: Median price of all brands and body types

Figure 3: Median price of MY20 and MY21 of all brands and body types

In MY20 and MY21 (model years), the median price of these body types among 13 brands is over 50,000 except hatch and SUV. Nowadays, the hatch is becoming more popular for individuals working and shopping while the family prefer the larger space an SUV provides. However, it is terrible news for many small businesses because they often use larger body types, such as Van, Ute and Cab Chassis, and these body types are a lot more expansive than the older MY in the last plot.


Figure 4: Percentage change from all MY to MY20 and MY21

From figure 4, the median price of MY20&21 has at least 41% higher than the med price of old model years while the Wagon, People Mover and Sedan remain the fastest increment.


BMW

Figure 5: BMW median price and body type

Figure 6: BMW stacked med price for model and body type

Some conclusions for BMW:

1: In BMW, body type hatch and wagon have the lowest median price around $40,000, which is $10,000 lower than the third lowest body type convertible.

2: In every model category, the highest/luxury level (8 series, i8, M8 and X7) has the highest median price in its category of models. X is the only model making SUV, and wagon only appears in 3 Series and 5 Series.

3: Series model covers five body types except for SUV. The buyer who has a limited budget could consider the Series model first. However, the buyer who only interests in the BMW SUV body type should only pay attention to the X model.


Holden

Figure 7: Holden median price and body type

Figure 8: Holden stacked med price for model and body type

Some conclusions for Holden:

1: For Holden cars, hatch has the lowest median price among body types, which is around $13,000. Ute and Cab Chassis have the top two highest med price. In my memory, these two body types are popular and economical selections for handymen and builders.

2: Holdens models and body types are slightly messy. Some models (like Acadia & Trailblazer, Calais & Commodore) have similar pricing segments and the same body types. I wonder why Holden made so many model names and only a few of them are popular in the market like Commodore, Ute, Colorado and Cruze.


Mazda

Figure 9: Mazda median price and body type

Figure 10: Mazda stacked med price for model and body type

Some conclusions for Mazda:

1: Mazda pricing segments can be put into three groups: a: Hatch, Sedan and wagon ($ 20,000 - 22,000) b: SUV, Cab Chassis and Convertible ($ 30,000 - 34,000) c: Coupe and Ute ($ 38,000 to 40,000)

2: Mazda pricing strategy is a lot tidy compare to Holdens. Models 2,3 and 6 target the familys general use in the first body type group. CX model provides SUV cars only with various price ranges.


Volkswagen

Figure 11: Volkswagen median price and body type

Figure 12: Volkswagen median price of each body type

Figure 13: Volkswagen stacked med price for model and body type

Some conclusions for Volkswagen:

1: Volkswagen covers almost every body types! The bus has the highest med price at 76,990. Surprisingly, the med prices of Convertible and Coupe are relatively low, which is against my intuition. This is because the model years of these two body types are from 2011 to 2014. Hatch, Sedan and Wagon have the med price close to or lower than 25,000 AUD. It makes these three body types the ideal types for students and new graduates who have a limited budget.

2: Those who interest in the Hatch, Sedan and Wagon with a limited budget, can pay some attention to the models including Polo, Beetle, Golf and Passat. Golf and Passat also provide some decent options for SUV type, and families with a sufficient budget could consider higher quality, such as T-Cross, T-Roc, Tiguan and Touareg.


Toyota

Figure 14: Toyota median price and body type

Figure 15: Toyota median price of each body type

Figure 16: Toyota stacked med price for model and body type

Figure 17: Mean Prices of Toyota and other Brands

Some conclusions for Toyota:

1: Toyota also provides a wide range of body types and price segments. Below 25,000 AUD, people can chose Hatch, Sedan and Wagon. You might realise that the third quartiles for these three body types are slightly larger than the second quartiles. This could imply the increment of med prices in Toyota MY21 cars as introduced in the last post.

2: From figure 13, Toyota invests most of its resources into three body types: People Mover, Sedan and SUV, and the order of price for these three body types from high to low is People Mover, SUV and Sedan. Same as the other brands, Hatch and Wagon are generally below 27,000 AUD. It makes them the best affordable body types for young generations. Hiace is a popular Toyota model focusing on the multi-purpose, and it has four body type options with reasonable price ranges.

3: Six years ago before I bought my first car, many people told me Toyota cars often keep their value. Because of this, my first car was a 2002 Camry. To proof this conclusion right, I made figure 17. In figure 17, The average price of Toyota cars remains constant between 25,000 and 30,000 until MY2019. Model years after 2019 exhibit incredible increments compare to the former MYs. For the other brands, the average price shows a linear increase from MY11 to MY19 following by an increment for MY20 and a huge fall for MY21. Base on this figure, I suspect the idea that Toyota cars can still keep their value in a long-term period after MY20.
